The Future of Energy: Nuclear Power and Greenhouse Gas Across the World

By Avery Karlin and Lucas Weiner

Why?

Our data is international results for both greenhouse gas emissions and nuclear power plants, per country. It is also juxtaposed to the GDP for these countries, both per Capita and gross. This comparison will not only show how various countries contribute to global climate change and whether or not nuclear power plants do enough to offset that, but the GDPs will also give us a sense of position within the global economy. Climate change is a serious issue, and it will be helpful to make progress towards understanding which countries are the issue, what they are doing about it, what they can do about it, and how effective that has been.
